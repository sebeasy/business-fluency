1. Nouns and Articles

English Grammar A1

  • Singular and plural nouns: Add -s or -es to make plurals (e.g., report → reports, meeting → meetings). Irregular: person → people.
  • Definite and indefinite articles: "The" for definite, "a/an" for indefinite singular. No gender. "Some" for plural indefinite.
  • Countable and uncountable nouns: Countable take a/an or numbers; uncountable take "some/any/much" (e.g., information, equipment).

Examples in English

  • Singular/plural: The client signs → The clients sign.
  • Articles: A project, The document, Some reports.
  • Uncountable: I need information, There’s no time.

Spanish vs. English Differences

  • No gender: English has no masculine/feminine nouns. Always use "the" (not el/la). E.g., la oficina → the office.
  • Article omission: English often drops articles with general ideas. E.g., Me gusta el café → I like coffee (no "the").
  • Plural indefinite: English usually omits "some." E.g., Unos clientes vienen → Clients are coming.

Tips

In Chile you say "pa’l cine" (para el cine), but in English always say "to the office" or "to the meeting." Never skip "the" when it’s needed!

2. Pronouns

English Grammar A1

  • Subject pronouns: I, you, he/she/it, we, you (plural), they.
  • Possessive adjectives: my, your, his/her/its, our, your, their (no agreement).
  • Demonstratives: this/that (singular), these/those (plural).
  • Object pronouns: me, you, him/her/it, us, you, them.

Examples in English

  • Subject: I work, She presents.
  • Possessive: My colleague (mi colega), His laptop (su laptop).
  • Demonstrative: This report is clear, Those emails are urgent.
  • Object: I call you (Te llamo), I send it (Lo envío – lo = informe).

Spanish vs. English Differences

  • Always use subject pronouns: Spanish drops them (Trabajo = I work), but English needs "I."
  • No gender in possessives: "My" works for masculine/feminine/plural (mis oficinas = my offices).
  • Object pronouns: English "it" = lo/la. No gender distinction for things.

Tip

"Su" can mean his/her/their – English is clearer! Always say "I work" (never just "work"). In Chile we drop "tú," but English needs "you."

3. Verbs

English Grammar A1

  • Present simple: Habits, facts (I/you/we/they work; he/she/it works).
  • Present continuous: Actions now (am/is/are + -ing).
  • Can / have to: Modal verbs (can attend, have to prepare).
  • Imperatives: Base verb (Come here, Don’t be late).
  • Common irregular: go-went, have-has, do-does.

Examples in English

  • Present simple: I work in Santiago, She doesn’t attend meetings.
  • Present continuous: I’m writing an email now.
  • Can: I can join the call.
  • Imperative: Listen! Don’t forget the deadline.

Spanish vs. English Differences

  • One "to be": English uses "be" for everything. Soy gerente = I am a manager (permanent). Estoy ocupado = I am busy (temporary). Same verb!
  • Third person -s: Only he/she/it adds -s (trabaja → works).
  • Do/does for questions & negatives: ¿Trabajas? → Do you work? No trabajo → I don’t work.
  • Continuous tense: English uses -ing more often. Estoy escribiendo → I’m writing (right now).

Tip

Never say "I am manager" with estar! Use examples: "Soy de Concepción" → I’m from Concepción (permanent). "Estoy en reunión" → I’m in a meeting (now). Chilean "estái" = "you are" → always write "you are."

4. Adjectives

English Grammar A1

  • Position: Before the noun (a busy office).
  • No agreement: Same adjective for all nouns (new project, new projects).
  • Comparatives: -er / more… (faster, more efficient).

Examples in English

  • A short meeting, An important email.
  • The blue laptops, The white printers.

Spanish vs. English Differences

  • Position: English: adjective + noun. Spanish: noun + adjective. Una reunión corta → a short meeting.
  • No gender/number change: "Fast" stays fast (rápido/rápida → fast).

Tip for Chilean Students:

Don’t say "an email important." Say "an important email." Practice: un informe claro → a clear report.

5. Adverbs

English Grammar at A1

  • Frequency: always, usually, sometimes, never.
  • Manner: well, quickly, carefully (often + -ly).

Examples in English

  • I never miss deadlines.
  • She presents well.

Spanish vs. English Differences

  • Position: Frequency before main verb (I always check). Manner usually after (She works quickly).
  • No -mente: rápido → quickly. bien → well (not "goodly").

Tip:

Don’t invent "goodly." Say "well." "Siempre reviso el informe" → I always check the report.

6. Prepositions

English Grammar A1

  • Place: in, on, at, under, next to.
  • Time: at (time), on (days), in (months/seasons).
  • Movement: to, from, into.

Examples in English

  • The printer is next to the desk.
  • Meeting at nine.
  • I’m going from the Valparaíso office.

Spanish vs. English Differences

  • In vs. at: En Viña = in Viña. A las nueve = at nine.
  • Phrasal verbs: "Log in," "fill out" – no equivalent in Spanish.

Tip

Never say "in Monday." Say "on Monday." "En la oficina" → in the office / at the office (context).

7. Sentence Structure

English Grammar A1

  • Word order: Subject + Verb + Object (strict).
  • Questions: Do/Does + subject + base verb.
  • Negatives: Don’t / Doesn’t + base verb.
  • Conjunctions: and, but, or.
